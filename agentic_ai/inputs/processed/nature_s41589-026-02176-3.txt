[SECTION: Abstract]
Here we invented an assay called 'SpyTag-Catcher Immobilization of Lanmodulin for Assaying Metal-Binding Selectivity' (SpyCI-LAMBS) to measure metalloprotein selectivity en masse. This 96-format workflow was used to study the selectivity of 621 lanmodulin (LanM) orthologs for 15 REs, revealing eight distinct selectivity profiles based on sequence-to-function analyses. We discovered >200 LanMs with stronger selectivity against low-value LaIII relative to the prototypical LanM. This includes a LanM that can perform a challenging one-stage separation of PrIII from LaIII with up to >99.9 mol% purity and 83% yield.

[SECTION: Introduction]
The lanthanide-binding protein lanmodulin (LanM) from Methylobacterium extorquens (Mex-LanM) has emerged as a promising candidate for RE purification. Mex-LanM exhibits extraordinarily high selectivity for REs5,6,17 and sufficient intra-RE selectivity when immobilized in column format to achieve individual separation among some RE pairs (for example, NdIII versus DyIII)6,11. However, Mex-LanM exhibits similar affinity for CeIII through EuIII, which limits the separation potential across light REs (LRE; LaIII–SmIII)11,17,18. A recent effort identified Hans-LanM from Hansschlegelia quercus in a bioinformatic campaign that unearthed 696 putative LanM orthologs19. Hans-LanM exhibits lanthanide-dependent dimerization and substantially enhanced selectivity for LaIII to NdIII compared to heavy REs (HREs; EuIII–LuIII, YIII). When immobilized, Hans-LanM enabled single-stage separation of a 95:5 NdIII/DyIII solution19.

[SECTION: Introduction]
We then translated the principles of SpyCI-LAMBS to column format in a method we named 'SpyCI-LAMBS for Estimating Chromatographic Separations' (SpECS) to enable screening of separation potential for a panel of orthologs using high aspect ratio (AR) columns. This identified a LanM ortholog from Methylobacterium sp.13MFTsu3.1M2 (called 'o-36') that achieved single-stage separation of the near-adjacent PrIII/LaIII pair, a challenging separation for Mex-LanM and Hans-LanM.

[SECTION: Results — Snapshots of the LanM selectivity landscape]
In our prior work, 696 putative LanM orthologs were identified using a PSI-BLAST against the NCBI database and filtering for sequences with <200 residues, 4 EF-hands in total and at least 1 pair of EF-hands separated by <14 residues19. From this set, we extracted a subset of 621 orthologs (herein termed the 'library') after manually removing redundant 4 EF-hand sequences and re-adding 3 EF-hand orthologs (Supplementary Data 1). The identity of each ortholog is denoted as 'o-X', where 'X' demarks the list position indexed at 0 (for example, o-180 is Hans-LanM, and o-621 is Mex-LanM).

[SECTION: Results — LanM selectivity is consistent on-resin and in-solution]
To investigate whether this notion held true across the selectivity clusters, we purified representative proteins (o-412, C1; o-543, C2; o-585, C3; o-36 and o-127, C5) and studied their binding properties in-solution with several REs.

[SECTION: Results — LanM selectivity is consistent on-resin and in-solution]
Using CD, we indeed found that o-412 had similar relative affinities for the LREs (1.6-fold difference between PrIII and LaIII), and weaker relative affinity for HREs (6.2-fold difference between PrIII and DyIII) compared to 4.7-fold difference between the similarly distant (among the LnIII series) NdIII and HoIII for Mex-LanM17. The C5 members o-36 and o-127 were of particular interest, due to their steeper selectivity against LaIII according to SpyCI-LAMBS. We used spectrofluorometry to allow direct comparison of these proteins. Both LanMs displayed notably weaker relative affinity for LaIII than that observed for Mex-LanM and for the monomeric version of Hans-LanM (R100K; Fig. 5b,c and Supplementary Table 2); o-36 had a striking 8.7-fold difference in affinity between NdIII and LaIII, whereas this difference was 3.5-fold for o-127 and 1.7-fold for Mex-LanM.

[SECTION: Results — C5 LanMs reject lanthanum for enhanced RE separations]
From each cluster, we initially chose members with the highest Mtot values (Fig. 2c). We then picked the final five candidates based on their high soluble protein expression levels and unique separation factor (SF) heatmaps (Supplementary Figs. 29–34)—o-25 (C7) from Methylobacterium aerolatum, o-36 (C5) from Methylobacterium sp.13MFTsu3.1M2, o-40 (C5) from Methylobacterium sp.P1-11, o-257 (C1) from Bradyrhizobium japonicum and o-380 (C4) from Methylopila sp.73B (Extended Data Fig. 5 and Supplementary Fig. 35).

[SECTION: Results — C5 LanMs reject lanthanum for enhanced RE separations]
We then purified o-36 and o-621 with a -GSGC tag to fabricate 0.8-ml agarose columns with a confirmed 2 μmolRE ml−1agarose capacity, determined by SmIII breakthrough (Supplementary Fig. 39). o-36's logD profile determined using the traditional column approach was nearly identical to the profile determined using SpyCI-LAMBS, especially in the LaIII–NdIII region (Supplementary Fig. 38). Both columns were fed a binary 50:50 solution of LaIII and SmIII with bound REs eluted using a two-step pH gradient (Fig. 6c,d, Extended Data Fig. 7 and Supplementary Fig. 40). o-36 achieved 94.7 mol% La purity at 97.2% yield, and 99.2 mol% Sm purity at 88.8% yield, whereas o-621 could not achieve separation of these two REs under the same conditions. Motivated by this result, we tested the more challenging PrIII/LaIII separation using a 50:50 binary solution of the two REs. With a single step-drop from pH 2.4 to 1.5, o-36 achieved >99.9 mol% PrIII with 83.2% yield. To confirm this ability to reject LaIII was reproducible, we refabricated new 0.4-ml agarose columns with a new batch of proteins. Without optimization of the elution timings and pH increments to accommodate the smaller scale, we still achieved a 98.6 ± 1.1 mol% SmIII purity with a 72.6 ± 1.1% yield, and 96.5 ± 0.2 mol% PrIII purity with a 72.0 ± 5.8% yield (Extended Data Fig. 8). In light of this demonstration, we name o-36 as 'Melba-LanM', a prototypical representative of the C5 selectivity cluster that reproducibly overcomes the LRE selectivity plateau of Mex-LanM and Hans-LanM.

[SECTION: Discussion]
While the LRE SFs of Melba-LanM are comparable with those for a recently discovered and further engineered protein, Mex-LanD–E75Q/E78A (SFPr/La of 5.1), which enriches for NdIII/PrIII over LaIII through selective dimerization27, the compatibility of Melba-LanM with a column-based process enables the propagation of its SFs over multiple theoretical plates, resulting in effective intra-LRE ion separation. Additional work would be required to leverage the dimerization mechanism of LanD on-column to facilitate a direct comparison of separation performance. In other recent work, HEW5 from Nocardioides zeae was shown to achieve a 50:50 binary separation of a LaIII/NdIII solution with >90% purity and 90% yield16.

[SECTION: Discussion]
For example, C1 orthologs like o-412 (from Methyloligella sp. GL2) have selectivity maxima centered around NdIII and greater selectivity against DyIII than Mex-LanM. When immobilized onto agarose, o-412 had an SFNd/Dy of 10.5 (Supplementary Table 3) compared to the reported 13.7 for Hans-LanM (R100K)19. In addition to having high intra-LRE selectivity, some C5 members (from MBIV) have similar or even higher NdIII/DyIII selectivity compared to Mex-LanM (Supplementary Fig. 21), which we confirmed through biochemical characterization of o-127.

[SECTION: Discussion]
It is known that Mex-LanM deploys a nine-coordinate geometry to bind to NdIII (Protein Data Bank (PDB) ID: 8FNS), whereas Hans-LanM uses a ten-coordinate geometry for LaIII and nine-coordinate geometry for DyIII (PDB ID: 8DQ2).

[SECTION: Methods — Expression and purification of untagged LanMs (for in-solution characterization)]
o-543 (Ensifer adhaerens), o-585 (Rhizobium sp. BK275), o-412 (Methyloligella sp. GL2), o-127 (Methylosinus sp. sav-2) and o-36 (Methylobacterium sp. 13MFTsu3.1M2), with predicted signal peptides removed and codon-optimized for expression in E. coli, were synthesized and purchased from Twist Biosciences in pET-29b. These proteins were expressed as described for Mex-LanM25 with the only modification being that the IPTG induction time was 4 h. Purification of all proteins followed the procedure for Mex-LanM25, except that the high-salt Q-Sepharose buffer (buffer M2) contained 600 mM NaCl instead of 1 M NaCl. Proteins were stored at −80 °C until further use. The yields of purified LanM proteins were 40 mg l−1 of culture (o-543), 70 mg l−1 of culture (o-585), 30 mg l−1 of culture (o-127), 40 mg l−1 of culture (o-412) and 20 mg l−1 of culture (o-36). Purification of o-21 (Methylobacterium sp. E-005) was also attempted, but its level of expression was low and was not obtained in sufficient purity for detailed characterization.
